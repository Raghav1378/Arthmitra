RBI Master Direction - Know Your Customer (KYC) Direction, 2016 (Updated 2023)

Chapter I - PRELIMINARY

1. Short Title and Commencement
(a) These Directions shall be called the Reserve Bank of India (Know Your Customer (KYC)) Directions, 2016.
(b) These directions shall come into effect on the same day as the Prevention of Money-laundering (Maintenance of Records) Rules, 2005.

Chapter VI - Customer Acceptance Policy

10. Customer Acceptance Policy
Regulated Entities (REs) shall frame a Customer Acceptance Policy.
(a) No account is opened in anonymous or fictitious/benami name.
(b) No account is opened where the RE is unable to apply appropriate Customer Due Diligence (CDD) measures, either due to non-cooperation of the customer or non-reliability of the documents/information furnished by the customer.
(c) No transaction or account-based relationship is undertaken without following the CDD procedure.
(d) The mandatory information to be sought for KYC purpose while opening an account and during the periodic update, is specified.
(e) Periodic Updating of KYC:
    (i) For High Risk customers, KYC must be updated every 2 years.
    (ii) For Medium Risk customers, KYC must be updated every 8 years.
    (iii) For Low Risk customers, KYC must be updated every 10 years.

11. Risk Management
Table 1: Risk Categorization
Risk Level | Examples
High Risk  | Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs), Non-residents, High Net Worth Individuals.
Medium Risk| Salaried employees with variable income structure.
Low Risk   | Salaried employees with salary crediting into account, Government departments.

Chapter IX - Operation of Accounts

23. Freezing of Accounts
If a customer fails to comply with KYC requirements, the bank may partially freeze the account after giving 3 months notice.
Total freezing allows for no credits or debits.
