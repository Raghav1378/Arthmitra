CYBER SAFETY GUIDELINES FOR BANKING CUSTOMERS
Issued by National Cyber Security Coordinator

1. Password & PIN Security
(a) Never share your UPI PIN, ATM PIN, or NetBanking Password with anyone, including bank officials. Banks never ask for these details.
(b) Do not write PINs on the back of cards.
(c) Change passwords every 90 days. use complex passwords (alphanumeric + special chars).

2. Phishing & Vishing Attacks
(a) Do not click on links in SMS/Emails claiming "KYC Suspended" or "Account Blocked". Validate by visiting the official bank branch or website.
(b) Check the URL carefully. Scammers use look-alike domains (e.g., 'hdfc-bank-verify.com' instead of 'hdfcbank.com').
(c) Call confirming a lottery win or job offer asking for a 'processing fee' is always a scam (Advance Fee Fraud).

3. UPI Safety (Unified Payments Interface)
(a) MPIN is ONLY needed to SEND money. You never need to enter your PIN to RECEIVE money.
(b) Avoid "Collect Requests" from strangers.
(c) Verify the identity of the receiver (Name matching) before sending large amounts.

4. Reporting Fraud
(a) Immediate Action: Call 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline) immediately if money is deducted unauthorizedly.
(b) Golden Hour: Reporting within 1 hour significantly increases chances of freezing the money before it move to mule accounts.
(c) File a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.
