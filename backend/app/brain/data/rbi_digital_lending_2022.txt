RBI GUIDELINES ON DIGITAL LENDING, 2022

1. Key Disclosures (Key Fact Statement)
All digital lending apps (DLAs) must provide a Key Fact Statement (KFS) to the borrower before the execution of the contract. The KFS must contain:
(a) The All-Inclusive Cost of Digital Loans in the form of an Annual Percentage Rate (APR).
(b) The recovery mechanism.
(c) Details of the grievance redressal officer.
(d) Cooling-off/look-up period.

2. Disbursement and Repayment
(a) All loan disbursements and repayments are required to be executed only between the bank accounts of the borrower and the Regulated Entity (RE).
(b) No pass-through account or pool account of the Lending Service Provider (LSP) or any third party is permitted.

3. Data Privacy
(a) DLAs can only store minimal data necessary for operations.
(b) Clear consent must be obtained for any data access.
(c) Access to mobile phone resources like file and media, contact list, call logs, and telephony functions is PROHIBITED.
(d) One-time access can be taken for camera, microphone, or location only for KYC purposes.

4. Cooling-off Period
(a) A cooling-off period shall be provided during which borrowers can exit the loan by paying the principal and the proportionate APR without any penalty.
(b) Minimum period: 3 days for loans having tenure of 7 days or more; 1 day for loans having tenure less than 7 days.
